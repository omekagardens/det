================================================================================
PART 3: CALIBRATION
================================================================================

1. MEASURE TORSION CONSTANT (κ_fiber):
   
   a. Displace the pendulum slightly and release
   b. Time the oscillation period T (average 10+ swings)
   c. Calculate: κ_fiber = 4π² I / T²
   
   where I = moment of inertia = 2 m L²
         m = small mass (kg)
         L = half-length of bar (m)

2. ESTABLISH LASER SCALE:
   
   a. Measure laser throw distance D (meters to wall)
   b. Angular deflection θ = Δx / (2D)  [factor of 2 from reflection]
   c. Torque = κ_fiber × θ
   d. Force = Torque / L

================================================================================
PART 4: MEASUREMENT PROCEDURE
================================================================================

1. Let system equilibrate (1-2 hours minimum)
2. Mark initial laser spot position
3. Move large masses to distance r from small masses
4. Wait for new equilibrium (30-60 minutes)
5. Measure laser deflection Δx
6. Calculate force: F = κ_fiber × θ / L
7. Repeat at different distances: r = 5, 8, 10, 12, 15, 20 cm

PART 7: TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

PROBLEM: No visible deflection
→ Use more massive source masses
→ Check fiber isn't too stiff
→ Reduce air currents (better enclosure)

PROBLEM: Pendulum drifts continuously  
→ Wait longer for equilibration
→ Check for thermal gradients
→ Ensure symmetry in mass placement

PROBLEM: Force doesn't scale as 1/r²
→ Check for magnetic interference
→ Verify masses aren't touching
→ Account for extended mass geometry
